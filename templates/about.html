Jacks or Better Has a Cool History


While Jacks or Better has been in casinos since the 1970s, it has much-richer history than this.

The game's origins can be traced back to the early 1800s, when poker was widely played on

Mississippi riverboats.






Author J.H. Green was the first person to coin the term Jacks or Better, doing so in 1834.

During his riverboat travels, Green wrote that Jacks or Better has fair rules, versus the

cheating-prone Three-Card Monte.






Aside from Green's reference, it's unclear when JoB originated.






The most-accurate timeline of Jacks or Better history begins in the 1970s, when the game first

appeared in casino floors.






But it wasn't until Si Redd came along in the mid-1970s that JoB became a popular game.






Redd's company, SIRCOMA (Si Redd's Coin Machines), had mild success with Draw Poker.

The problem with their 5-card variant is that players must get a two pair or better to win.






Redd changed the lowest payout to a pair of jacks so that players would win with greater frequency.

The move was a genius decision that helped the video poker's popularity immensely.
